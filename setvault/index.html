The idea

A digital album you actually own

Most collectors keep their coins in a Whitman-style folder — a page of labeled slots you fill over the years. Setvault brings that ritual to your phone without taking anything away from you: your coins stay yours, your photos stay on your device, and your collection is never locked into someone else's cloud.

Built around self-custody

Everything is stored encrypted on your device. There is no account to create and no server that holds your data. When you want a backup, you export a single .setvault file and keep it on the drive you trust. That is the whole promise: you hold your collection, on storage you choose.

Facts you can rely on

The dates, mint marks, mintages and specifications come from public-domain sources and are cross-checked before they ship. Numismatic accuracy is the point — so the app never guesses and never invents a mintage.

Frequently asked

Is Setvault free?
Yes. The Washington Quarter — 50 State Quarters album is free, so you can explore the whole app. A single one-time payment of $19.99 — the Full version — unlocks the other nine U.S. series and everything else: all eight bindings and themes, statistics and advanced export. There is no subscription and there are no ads.
Does Setvault use the cloud?
No. Everything is stored encrypted on your device with SQLCipher. There is no account, no cloud and no analytics. You back up your collection to a single .setvault file that you keep wherever you trust.
Can Setvault identify a coin from a photo?
No, and that is on purpose. Your photo is a record of the coin you own, not an AI guess. You choose the slot in the album, and Setvault provides the historical facts.
Where do the coin facts come from?
From public-domain sources such as the U.S. Mint, cross-checked against independent references. Facts are not copyrightable, and we never invent mintages or specifications.
Which coins are included?
Setvault launches with ten United States series: Washington Quarter — 50 State Quarters (free), Lincoln Cent (Wheat and Memorial), Buffalo Nickel, Jefferson Nickel, Mercury Dime, Roosevelt Dime, Washington Quarter (1932–1998), Kennedy Half Dollar and Morgan Dollar. More series are on the way — tell us which one you'd like next.
What happens to my collection if I lose my phone?
You export an encrypted .setvault backup file and keep it wherever you like. Importing it restores everything, photos included. Basic export is always free — we never hold your data hostage.
